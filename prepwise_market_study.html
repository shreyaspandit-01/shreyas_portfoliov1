01 — Market Context

Why Interview Prep Is a Real Market

Interview prep has historically been a informal, word-of-mouth business — seniors helping juniors, peer mock sessions, the occasional coaching class. That's changing. The formalisation of PM recruiting in India, the emergence of structured consulting placement seasons at IIMs, and the proliferation of APM programs at funded startups have turned interview preparation into a definable, payable need.

Candidates know the stakes. A PM role at CRED or Zepto is not just a job — it's a career-defining moment that shapes trajectory for years. Consulting placements at MBB from IIMs determine where you spend the first decade of your career. The willingness to pay for preparation is high and growing.

$6.3B
Global interview prep and career coaching market projected by 2031.
India is the fastest-growing segment — driven by PM role expansion and consulting placement culture.

What's Driving Growth

Three forces are converging. First, the number of PM roles in India has expanded significantly — every funded startup, every enterprise company digitising operations needs product managers, and supply hasn't kept up with demand. Second, the bar has risen: companies now have structured interview processes, company-specific rubrics, and multiple rounds. Third, LLM APIs have made it possible to build company-specific, persona-driven simulations that were technically infeasible two years ago.

The B2B multiplier: SOM calculations for B2C platforms systematically underestimate value when a B2B revenue layer exists. PrepWise's B2B talent pipeline — companies paying to access pre-scored candidate pools — is not in the SAM calculation above. It significantly expands the revenue ceiling beyond pure subscription economics.
03 — Competitive Landscape

Who Else Is Playing

The interview prep market has players, but none of them combine what PrepWise combines. The landscape splits into two categories: content platforms (question banks, video courses, written guides) and simulation tools (human mocks, AI tools). Content platforms have depth but no simulation. Simulation tools have interaction but no company-specific depth.

That bet is validated by how candidates actually spend the 2 weeks before an interview.

04 — Go-to-Market Strategy

How PrepWise Reaches Candidates

PrepWise's GTM is sequenced around the India market first — where the company library is deepest and the PMF signal is strongest — before expanding globally. Each phase builds the data and community assets that make the next phase cheaper to execute.

1
India
India Wedge — PM Candidates at Target Companies
Direct outreach to candidates applying to the 9 live companies. LinkedIn content demonstrating PrepWise's company-specific depth — teardown posts, session screenshots (with permission), Readiness Score case studies. Campus presence at IITs and Delhi University. The India company library is an unfair advantage no global tool can quickly replicate.
2
Consult
Consulting Track Launch — IIM Placement Season
Target IIM students in the 6 months before placement season. Consulting prep is a known, documented need — the MBB track gives PrepWise credibility with the highest-demand segment. Partnerships with student placement committees. Word-of-mouth is especially strong in this cohort — if one person in a study group uses PrepWise, all five will.
3
Global
Global Pool Unlock — Target Mode for Any Company
When Target Mode is live, PrepWise works for any company on earth — no new rubric required. This is the global unlock moment. Distribution shifts to product-led growth: candidates who use Target Mode for their specific interview share results, which drives organic acquisition from candidates at the same company. Each successful interview is a word-of-mouth event.
4
B2B
B2B Talent Pipeline — Companies as Customers
Once PrepWise has sufficient Readiness Score data across a meaningful candidate pool, companies pay to access pre-filtered, rubric-matched candidate pipelines. The pitch is simple: instead of screening 500 resumes, here are 20 candidates who've already been evaluated against your exact rubric. A conversion rate improvement of even 10% on hiring makes the subscription cost trivially small for most companies.
05 — Risks and Mitigations

What Could Go Wrong

Large competitor enters India PM prep with company depth
Exponent or a well-funded Indian startup builds 10+ Indian company rubrics and AI simulation. The moat narrows.
Medium
Mitigation: session data moat compounds. Readiness Score data, blind spot detection, and cross-session intelligence can't be copied quickly — they require months of real user data to be meaningful.
LLM costs make the unit economics unworkable
Claude API costs per session exceed what ₹199/month can support at scale, especially as session quality improves with Sonnet.
Medium
Mitigation: session efficiency improvements (arc limits reduce token count), tiered pricing for power users, B2B revenue subsidising B2C cost structure.
Candidates don't convert from free to paid
The free tier provides enough value that users don't upgrade. Free-to-paid conversion stays below 10%.
Low
Mitigation: Readiness Score persistence, cross-session comparison, and bookmarked transcript replay are all paid-only features. The value of continued use is structurally paywalled.
Indian company rubrics become stale
Companies change their interview formats. PrepWise's rubrics fall behind the actual bar — candidates who use PrepWise are under-prepared for how companies actually interview now.
Low
Mitigation: community feedback loop. PrepWise users who go through real interviews can report format changes. Quarterly rubric reviews for all live companies.
06 — Conclusion

The Case for PrepWise

The interview prep market is real, growing, and underserved for the specific tracks PrepWise targets. The tools that exist are either too generic (question banks, video courses) or too expensive and time-bound (human cohorts). The gap between them — on-demand, company-specific, AI-powered simulation with persistent skill tracking — is precisely where PrepWise sits.

The India wedge is not a limitation. It's the entry point. The deepest Indian startup rubric library of any platform is a genuine, compounding moat. And when Target Mode goes live, PrepWise becomes a global product without requiring a single additional rubric to be hardcoded.

The structural bet: Preparation and hiring are not two separate markets. They're two sides of the same candidate journey. PrepWise is the first platform building toward a position that serves both — and that's the business that has no direct competitor today, and will have a structural head start on any competitor that tries to build it tomorrow.
