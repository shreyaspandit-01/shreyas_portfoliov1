01 — Overview

Product Overview

PrepWise is a live AI interview simulation platform. Candidates enter a session by selecting their track, choosing a company, and providing their JD and resume. A company-specific AI architect then runs a structured interview — with real probing, real pushback, and feedback calibrated to that company's actual rubric.

The session follows a four-moment arc that mirrors how real interviews are structured. Every session ends resolved. Post-session, candidates receive an annotated transcript and a Readiness Score update across six skill dimensions that persists across all future sessions.

Design principle: The session is the product. Everything else — Readiness Score, leaderboard, B2B pipeline — is built on top of what happens inside a session. If the session feels like a quiz, PrepWise is a quiz app. If it feels like a real interview, it's a career platform.
02 — Session Architecture

The Four-Moment Session

Every PrepWise session — across all tracks, all companies, all modes — follows four distinct moments. Each moment has a specific purpose, a specific duration, and a specific thing it trains. This architecture makes PrepWise feel like a real interview rather than a practice tool.

01
The Briefing
Before the interview starts, the user sees an Intel Card — a compact mission brief. Architect persona, company value signal, format preview, and expected duration. Sets expectations before the pressure begins.
~30 seconds · V2 Priority
02
The Opening
The architect introduces themselves, explains the format, and asks a warm-up opener — typically "tell me about yourself." Establishes the persona's voice and energy before the main case lands.
60–90 seconds · V2 Priority
03
The Case
The main event. Architect delivers the case conversationally, then probes — interrupting, redirecting, introducing constraints. 5–8 total exchanges, structured across the 6-stage case arc. Always resolves at Stage 6.
25–30 min · 5–8 exchanges · V2 Priority
04
The Close
Candidate asks questions. Architect responds in character. Scored — a candidate who says "no questions" has made a mistake. Ends with a clean resolution line from the architect before session wraps.
2 minutes · V2 Priority

The 6-Stage Case Arc

Within Moment 3, every case follows six stages regardless of track. The architect is stage-aware — it knows where it is and advances at the right moment. Hard exchange limits prevent the infinite follow-up loop that currently exists in V1.

StageWhat HappensExchange Limit
1 — ClarificationCandidate asks 1–2 smart clarifying questions. Architect answers concisely. If candidate dives in without clarifying — noted in feedback.Max 1
2 — StructuringCandidate lays out their framework. Architect probes the structure once — never circles back to structuring in later stages.1–2
3 — AnalysisMain case work. Architect introduces a new constraint or data point mid-analysis. Max 3 exchanges, then architect advances regardless.Max 3
4 — RecommendationCandidate lands their recommendation — clear, direct, with rationale. One exchange. Architect listens without interruption.1
5 — PushbackArchitect pushes back once — even on a good answer. Candidate defends. One exchange only. Architect accepts and closes after defence.1 — critical
6 — ResolutionArchitect signals case completion. Transition to Moment 4. Session ends. Feedback loads.—
Critical rule: The architect never probes the same dimension twice. Stage-aware behaviour and this rule together eliminate the infinite spiral problem in V1. Both the quick fix (exchange counter) and the proper fix (stage tracker) ship in V2.
03 — Feature Specifications

Core Features

In-Session Features

Live Dimension TrackerV2

A subtle sidebar showing the 6 Readiness Score dimensions lighting up in real time as the candidate covers them during the session. Structuring lights up when they structure. Metrics lights up when they quantify. Trains self-monitoring — candidates learn which dimensions they consistently skip.

Pause & Clarify ButtonV2

Candidate can pause at any point to ask a clarifying question. Asking the right clarifying question at the right moment is itself a skill PrepWise explicitly teaches. Usage is tracked and reflected in session feedback.

Moment BookmarkingV2

A bookmark icon candidates tap during the session to flag moments they want to revisit in feedback. Makes post-session review targeted — candidates come in knowing exactly which moments to focus on.

Post-Session Transcript ReplayV2

Full session conversation with the architect's internal assessment of each response shown inline — colour-coded by dimension. Green = strong. Amber = acceptable. Red = needs work. The single most powerful post-session feature. Turns a session from an experience into a learning tool.

Voice ModeV2

Web Speech API for candidate voice input. ElevenLabs TTS for architect voice output — different voice profiles per company archetype. Same four-moment arc, same assessment pipeline underneath. Adds filler word detection, speaking pace analysis, and pacing report to the post-session feedback.

Resume DNA EngineV3

Upload PDF resume → Claude extracts companies, roles, domains, seniority, project experience, skill signals. Cases reference actual background. Gap analysis vs target role JD. STAR story coaching built from the candidate's own projects, positioned for the specific company's rubric.

Readiness Score

The cross-session skill model that separates PrepWise from every competitor. Tracks performance across six dimensions across all sessions — calibrated against the target company's specific rubric. A 7.4 against Zepto's bar (speed-of-decision, ops thinking) is a different number from 7.4 against CRED's (design taste, emotional intelligence).

Metrics ThinkingKPI choice, quantification, metric diagnosisFlagged when candidate uses or avoids quantification
User EmpathyUser-centring, journey thinking, design reasoningPresence/absence of user framing in product answers
PrioritisationTradeoff reasoning, resource allocationQuality of Stage 4 recommendation and Stage 5 defence
Business SenseRevenue thinking, competitive positioningBusiness context in framing and recommendation

Consulting Track — V2

Covers MBB (McKinsey interviewer-led, BCG/Bain candidate-led), Big 4 Strategy, and In-house Strategy at Indian conglomerates. The four-moment arc maps directly to how consulting interviews are structured — the case arc is even more standardised than PM interviews, making simulation fidelity more achievable.

V2 scope decision: McKinsey and BCG/Bain ship first. Big 4 and In-house Strategy ship as V2.1 and V2.2 respectively. The distinction between McKinsey's interviewer-led format and BCG's candidate-led format requires different architect behaviours and ships as two separate modes.
05 — Product Modes

Practice Mode & Target Mode

Practice Mode

For candidates building general interview foundations. Curated company pools — India (15 companies) and Global (25 companies across 8 archetypes). Candidate picks track, pool, company, difficulty, and case type. Session begins. Readiness Score updates after every session.

Target Mode — The Global Unlock

For candidates with a real application. JD + company name + resume → real-time AI research → fully personalised session built around this specific company and this specific candidate's background. Works for any company on earth, not just the curated pools.

The key connection: Every job listing on the Jobs Page (V4) has a "Practice for this role" button. One tap → Target Mode launches pre-loaded with that JD and company name. The candidate uploads their resume and the interview begins. No other platform has this flow.

Architect never probes the same dimension twice within a session
✓
Architect pushback at Stage 5 fires exactly once for every session
✓
Post-session transcript replay with inline dimension colour-coding
✓
Consulting track live with McKinsey and BCG/Bain modes distinct
✓
Briefing Intel Card shown before every session
✓
Readiness Score updates after every session with dimension breakdown
