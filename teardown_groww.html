Teardown No. 02

Groww Made Investing Effortless.
That's Also Its Biggest Problem.

Groww's simplicity is a genuine product achievement. It brought millions of first-time investors into the market. But somewhere between the clean onboarding and the cheerful UI, the product forgot to grow with its users.

I've been using Groww for two years. Started with SIPs, moved into direct equity, experimented with F&O briefly, came back to mutual funds. I've watched the product improve in some ways and stagnate in others. Here's my honest read.

Groww entered a market dominated by apps that looked like they were built in 2009. Cluttered dashboards, confusing jargon, forms that felt like tax filings. Its bet was simple: if we make this feel like a consumer app, people will actually use it. That bet paid off. Groww grew to 10M+ users faster than almost any fintech in India.

But there's a problem with winning on simplicity — at some point your users get smarter, and your product has to get smarter with them. That's where Groww is stuck.

01 —
What Groww Actually Gets Right

Before the critique, the credit. Groww's onboarding is the best in the Indian fintech space. KYC to first investment in under 10 minutes. Clear language — "this is a mutual fund, here's what that means, here's what your money will do." No intimidating terminology before the user is ready for it. No dark patterns pushing demat accounts on people who just want a SIP.

What Groww Gets Right
Onboarding removes every possible friction point
Category labels written in plain Hindi-English — no jargon walls
SIP setup is genuinely 3 taps
Portfolio view is clean and readable at a glance
Returns are shown prominently — makes users feel good
Where It Falls Short
No progression path for users who want more depth
Fund comparison is weak — surface-level metrics only
Stock analysis tools are cosmetic, not analytical
No educational content that grows with the user
Notifications are marketing, not intelligence

The portfolio view deserves specific praise. Absolute returns, XIRR, invested vs current value — all visible without digging. That's a product decision. Most apps hide XIRR because it can look worse than absolute returns for short-term holders. Groww shows it anyway. That's honest.

02 —
The Simplicity Trap

There's a version of simplicity that's a philosophy and a version that's a ceiling. Groww started with the former and drifted into the latter.

When I search for a mutual fund on Groww, I see the fund name, 1Y/3Y/5Y returns, and a rating. That's it. I can't filter by expense ratio, exit load, fund manager track record, portfolio overlap with funds I already hold, or volatility profile. I can't compare two funds side by side with any meaningful analytical depth.

The miss: Groww designed its discovery experience for a user who doesn't know what they're doing. That's the right user to design for on day one. But it never built a second experience for the user who's been investing for 18 months and wants to make smarter decisions. That user currently leaves the app, does research on Morningstar or ValueResearch, then comes back to execute. Groww is capturing the transaction but losing the relationship.

I've done this myself. Countless times. I open Groww, see a fund, want to understand it better, open Chrome, look it up externally, close Chrome, come back to Groww to buy. That round-trip is an indictment of the product's depth. The research phase should never leave the app.

User journey today: Groww → external research → Groww (to buy) User journey it should be: Groww → Groww → Groww
03 —
The User Maturity Gap

Groww's user base has aged. The people who signed up in 2020-21 during the bull run are now two to four years into their investing journey. Many of them have outgrown what the app can teach them. But the product hasn't built a path for what comes next.

Beginner User (Year 1)
Needs: simple language, guided decisions, low stakes. Groww serves this user exceptionally well.
Intermediate User (Year 2+)
Needs: analytical tools, fund comparison, portfolio overlap detection. Groww does not serve this user.
Engaged Equity Trader
Needs: screeners, technical indicators, watchlist depth. Groww's equity tools are cosmetic at best.
Goal-Based Investor
Needs: projected corpus, scenario modelling, goal tracking. Groww has a goals feature that barely works.

The goals feature is the clearest example of this gap. Groww lets you label an SIP with a goal — "Home," "Retirement," "Child's education." But it doesn't model whether your current contributions will actually get you there. There's no projection engine, no scenario modelling, no "at this rate, here's when you'll reach your goal." It's a label on a transaction, not a financial planning tool.

The opportunity: Groww already has all the data it needs to build a genuinely useful goal-tracking experience. Investment history, SIP schedules, fund performance. The product surface just doesn't exist. This is a roadmap and prioritisation failure, not a data or technical constraint.
04 —
The Discovery Problem

Groww's notifications are almost entirely marketing. New fund NFO. Referral bonus. "Markets are up 2% today." Nothing that feels like it was written for me specifically — nothing that uses the fact that it knows my portfolio, my SIP amounts, my holding period, my risk profile.

Imagine instead: "Your Axis Bluechip SIP is 14 months old. The fund has underperformed its benchmark for the last 3 quarters. Here's what that means and what similar investors have done." Or: "You have ₹8,400 sitting idle in your Groww wallet. At your current allocation profile, here's where it might fit."

That's not sci-fi. That's just using the data Groww already has to build an intelligent nudge layer. The gap between what Groww knows about me and what it communicates to me is enormous. Every push notification is a missed opportunity to demonstrate that the product is paying attention.

The competitive risk: Zerodha, Kuvera, and INDMoney are all building toward this intelligence layer — personalised insights, portfolio analysis, advisor-quality nudges at scale. If Groww stays in "simple consumer app" mode while competitors build genuine financial intelligence tools, its best users will migrate upward. The users who stay will be the ones with the least engagement and the lowest LTV.

06 —
Final Take

Groww built the best onramp into investing that India has ever had. That's a real achievement, and it shouldn't be minimised. The product moved an entire generation of first-time investors from intimidation to action. That's hard. Most products don't manage it.

The challenge now is the highway after the onramp. Groww got millions of users into the car. But the road it built only goes for about 12 months before it runs out. The users who want to keep going — who want to understand their portfolio better, make smarter decisions, build toward real financial goals — currently have to get off the highway and find their own path.

The product Groww needs to build next isn't a new product — it's a deeper version of the one that already exists. The same clean interface, but with more intelligence underneath. The same simple language, but for more complex questions. Not a pivot. An evolution.

The users are ready. The question is whether Groww's product roadmap is.
